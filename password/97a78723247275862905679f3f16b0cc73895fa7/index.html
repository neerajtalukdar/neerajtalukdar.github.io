Agent Desktop Re-Design

Re-designing the Agent Experience
  • Categories: Competitor Analysis,UI Design, Usability Testing
  • Timeline: Jun 2019 - present
  • Team Members: Rajiv Krishnan (Product Manager), Mocha Wu (Visual Designer), Lily Pai (UX Designer)
  • Mentor: Prudvi Raj Dommaraju

What was my contribution?

I led online brainstorming sessions with the PM and other designers using Miro, an online collaboration tool. I defined lo-fidelity wireframes to iterate on the options and helped co-ordinate stakeholder reviews on the designs to finalize on the options. I collaborated with the other designers on the team to help define hi-fidelity of the design options.

Overview

The design explorations that this section talks about is based on the research conducted.

Research

We visited 5 sites and talked to 17 agents, 7 supervisors, 5 administrators and 5 report designers spanning 2 weeks and across mutiple cities - Phoenix, Atlanta, Dallas, Pittsburgh and Asheville. This project is under NDA. For more details around the study please reach out to me.

"We use so many modules…If we can get all that into one, or two places, that would be great "

Key Insights

- There is no one size fits all desktop. Customers want flexibility in defining their own experience. This becomes critical for customers who want to migrate from on-prem to cloud, as they have the ability to build and manage their own gadgets on-prem. Thus, there needs to be a way to have flexibility in building out the agent desktop in terms of integrations, widgets, layout they would want for their organization.

- There is no on-boarding experience at all. Agents have to undergo extensive training to get onboarded to the UI. Also this requires them to undergo everytime there is a feature update.

- Agents often use the agent desktop in conjunction with several other applications, websites, CRM systems etc. There is steep learning curve involved with this framework and agents get used to the multi-tab multi-window approach over a period of time. A pattern of usage observed was minimizing the view.

- A lot of the everyday interactions require agents to perform menial, monotonous tasks like searching for knowledge base articles and responses which takes up a lot of the agents' time.

- Overall experience is broken as agents use a bunch of different tool and applications to get to the relevant information about the customer and identifying the best possible way to resolve their query/issue.

- There is no proper way for agents to track their performance over time and get an undersatnding of how they are faring as compared to others, ways in which they can improve.
