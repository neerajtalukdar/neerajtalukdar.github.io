Google Answers

AI based services

Project Info

Project Members: Ryan Bloom (PM), Tony Deng (Architect)

Time: 2 months

Theme: Web Application, AI based service, Chat Interface

Problem Statement

Agents in Contact Centers deal with lots of customer queries of various kinds. Often times, they have to reference articles, documents and FAQs to resolve these issues. A lot of time is spent on actually searching for the relevant answer. Since most contact centers are technically "Cost Centers", it becomes critical to save time, and help agents in this process of answer retrieval.

Solution

We propose a smart device for measuring the weight and height of the children. The record goes to an app supervised by the anganwadi worker. As a result, the measurement is recorded and stored electronically in the central database avoiding erroneous data collection and can be used to analyze several health trends and provide for early intervention.

Key Highlights

The system design assumes that the center will deploy this at a large scale to achieve uniformity

The app has been designed in a way that the local residents can use it easily supporting local languages

Theme: Mobile App, Service Design

Theme: Mobile App, Service Design

Overview

Contact Centers support almost every single product or service that we use on an everyday basis. From the simplest to the most complicated product or service that you can think of, there is almost surely a customer support team set up to take care of your needs and answer your questions. The project I worked on was focused on the cloud offering for the persona of customer support agents. It primarily consisted of two major efforts:

1. Field visits conducted with 5 of our customers to understand their current experience and pain-points and also understand nuances around contact center in general.

2. Re-designing the agent desktop experience based on the learnings, studying trends of existing comptetitors and also aligning with the overall design system alignment across the Collaboration Technology Group in Cisco.

Research

We visited 5 sites and talked to 17 agents, 7 supervisors, 5 administrators and 5 report designers spanning 2 weeks and across mutiple cities - Phoenix, Atlanta, Dallas, Pittsburgh and Asheville. For more details around the study please reach out to me for more details as it is under NDA.

"We use so many modules…If we can get all that into one, or two places, that would be great "

Outcome

We identified 4 main efforts coming out of this research study:

1. Fixing short-term issues and gaps with the current agent desktop solution. The objective was to address some of the low-hanging fruits and also make strategic moves in the direction of new agent desktop experience being worked upon in the background. Also aligning the overall experience with that of the rest of Webex portfolio. One strategic move in that direction was to change the look and feel to align with the Momentum Design system. This was primarily supported by Mocha, the visual designer in the project, with myself providing guidance from an overall experience perspective ensuring that all the scenarios were being covered.

2. The re-imagination and conceptualization of a new agent desktop experience. This was based on the research findings, competitor analysis and also with the business objective of migration of an enormous customer base from the on-prem solution to the cloud solution. View details of the project here

3. An agent desktop layout builder experience to support partner customizations and integrations. This was mostly driven by Karen Song, an intern in our team. She led a few webex calls with partners and customers and started off with some initial explorations. I acted as a mentor for this sub-project in the initial phases and took over from her once she completed her internship. View details of the project here

4. Defining a supervisor desktop experience - who act as super agents and help manage teams, queues, analyze performance and provide guidance and coaching to agents. In the solution we inheritted, we did not have a consolidated experience as such. View details of the project here
