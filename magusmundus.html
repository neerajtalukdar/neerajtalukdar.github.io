We worked on the scenarios we considered most critical, including coordination among caregivers, remote health monitoring and care history sharing. We collectively worked on the stories and documented the selected set of scenarios as storyboards that communicate to an external audience how the app creates value for the personas involved.

Play Testing

Participants: 8th standard (20 students) Method of play-testing: 4 groups of 5 students each were formed for the study. Pre-tests, play-tests and post-tests were conducted for each group using: Direct Observation Verbal Reports Qusetions and Answers Prior knowledge of each group was tested using open ended questions about scientific nomenclature and diseases and their relevance with their course curriculum, endangered plants and medicinal plants. It was observed that all the students were familiar with Harry Potter and 6 of them could remember few of the spells used in Harry Potter. They became excited when discussed with about magic, spell and fantasy world. Overall their prior knowledge and demographics were best suited for our game. We performed free form play testing, but initial guidance about the controls and interface was provided to the students as there were no instructions about these. Data obtained: Observations were made to check where the players faced problems. Questions were asked to understand their perception, opinion and beliefs about the game play. Players were really immersed in the game play, which might be due to the story line. Another thing about their attitude is that they like healing sick people. They like socializing. So introduction of more social elements might make them feel better and giving them a visual cue about their social participation (other than the skill bar within the level) at the end of the level would help in the reflection of their social behavior more discretely. On being asked, few of them said that they would like healing more than shooting a witch. Keeping in mind our game objectives and the results of the play testing, we would like to have a triangular association between the scientific name, common name and disease. One to one associations between all of these 3 domains of knowledge are necessary from the learning objective focus and smooth game play. Children were specific about the characters. Few of them said that they would like an owl, one of them said that he would like to ride a horse and cure the people. This once again validates the need for the aesthetic of expression. When presented with the choice of killing the witch by typing the name and aiming and shooting, 14 students were in the favor of typing the name. Brute force: Players were trying to mug up the information about the plants that were shown to them. It might be due to the ill communication of the information that is required to play the game. But this problem might have developed due to the play test being conducted in the first level itself. It should not arise in the subsequent levels. There was another UI problem here: while trying to mug up the name, they were trying to spell the scientific name of the plant and they were having problem in this. This can be solved by an audio interaction which would play the scientific name of the plant once the mouse pointer is hovered over the button displaying information about the plant. UI problem: While playing the game, there was a problem with the cat. The cat’s speed was not complementing the player’s speed. Trail of the cat’s path and comparable speeds might solve this issue. The player waited sometime while healing a sick person with a specific disease for the first time. This proves that the player was trying to recall the names hard. Also they were persistent about finding the solution. So while encountering the sick person for the first time, we can provide him with a relevant hint. And we found from the posttest interview that the players could remember the common names more which might be due to their more familiarity with the common names in their natural environment. From the 2nd encounter with the same type of sickness, he could be shown the common name on making mistakes for the 3rd time in their selection of the correct choice
