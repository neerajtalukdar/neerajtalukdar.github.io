Desktop Re-design

In this project, I worked on re-designing the agent desktop web application based on customer feedback gathered during a field-research trip.
This project is under NDA. For more information please contact me directly.

Project Overview

This project is focused on re-defining the experience for contact center agents. We inherited a contact center solution as part of an acquisition of Broadsoft and the objective was to identify existing issues and re-design the product based on our design system and also improve the overall user experience of the product.

Solution

We identified key gaps in the existing experience based on the field visits with some of the customers. We did a visual re-skin of the existing solution as part of a braning effort. We also re-designed the user expereince of the product with the intention of replacing the legacy solution. The main focus was on re-designing the information architecture and layout and identify key features going forward.

Project Info

Timeline: March 2019 - current
Theme: Website, User Research
Contribution: I led the user research sessions with one other designer and 3 other PMs. I was responsible for doing synthesis and analysis of the data and finally coming up with an improved experience. My main focus was to build out the IA, layout and wire-frames and also think of ways to enhance the current expereince through feature improvements.

A TLDR version of the project

We visited 6 customers across different cities in the US to gather feedback on the existing product

We identified some key gaps and opportunity areas in the agent experience resulting in a significant change in the IA and Layout

It spawned two critical side efforts - Layout Builder and Supervisor Desktop

Let's start with some basics. What is a Contact Center?

Contact Centers support almost every single product or service that we use on an everyday basis. From the simplest to the most complicated product or service that you can think of, there is almost surely a customer support team set up to take care of your needs and answer your questions. These agents generally handle several customer support issues on a daily basis over calls, chat or emails. Everyone single one of us has most likely encountered an interaction with an agent.

Acquisition of Broadsoft

Cisco acquired Broadsoft in early 2018. This resulted in inheritence of a cloud based Contact Center solution. We already had a stroing foothold in the on-prem market so the solution helped in complementing our overall solution.

The Strategic Goals

Agent Desktop is the cream of any contact center solution. This is the interface that the agent uses to manage chat, email and voice interactions. Although there are other aspects like Admin interface, script builder, report designer. The objective was to get feedback on the existing experience and improve upon it such that we get maximum traction in the market.

Breakdown of the effort

The project I worked on was focused on the cloud offering for the persona of customer support agents. It primarily consisted of two major efforts:

1. Field visits conducted with 6 of our customers in the US to understand their current experience and pain-points and also understand nuances around contact center in general.

2. Analyzing the data from the field visit and re-designing the agent desktop experience based on the learnings, studying trends of existing comptetitors and also aligning with the overall design system alignment across the Collaboration Technology Group in Cisco. This evolved into 4 major design initatives.

i. Fixing short-term issues and gaps with the current agent desktop solution. The objective was to address some of the low-hanging fruits and also make strategic moves in the direction of new agent desktop experience being worked upon in the background. Also aligning the overall experience with that of the rest of Webex portfolio. One strategic move in that direction was to change the look and feel to align with the Momentum Design system. This was primarily supported by Mocha, the visual designer in the project, with myself providing guidance from an overall experience perspective ensuring that all the scenarios were being covered. Here is a link to a press release that happened around this effort.

WCC Desktop Visual Refresh

ii. The re-imagination and conceptualization of a new agent desktop experience. This was based on the research findings, competitor analysis and also with the business objective of migration of an enormous customer base from the on-prem solution to the cloud solution.

Agent Desktop Re-Design


iii. An agent desktop layout builder experience to support partner customizations and integrations. One major business objective was to bring in some of the flexibilities that was already available in our on-prem products. This was mostly driven by Karen Song, an intern in our team. She led a few webex calls with partners and customers and started off with some initial explorations. I acted as a mentor for this initiative over the course of the project. This work is currently at it's nascent stages.

Layout Builder


iv. Defining a supervisor desktop experience - who act as super agents and help manage teams, queues, analyze performance and provide guidance and coaching to agents. In the solution we inheritted, we did not have a consolidated experience as such. This work is currently at it's nascent stages.

Supervisor Desktop


Process Overview

I learnt a lot from this mammoth project. At a high level, this project helped develop leadership skills as I was responsible for leading a 3 member design team and collaborated with product managers, engineering managers and developers across multiple time-zones. This was a unique experience to conduct research at contact centers and travel with other product managers and deisgners in the team. We had a great time in engaging the product managers in user-centered research techniques. Assessment of the accumulated information with the team now dispersed across two different time-zones was also a challenge which we tried to overcome through online collaboration using Miro. Analyzing the research inputs and working with the product manager to define the product roadmap was a first time experience for me.
