Future of Contact Center

This projects covers a vision initiative that the UX team undertook as part of a leadership initiative to define the future of contact center experiences.
This project is under NDA. For more information please contact me directly.

Project Overview

With the acquisition of Broadsoft, Cisco had inherited a contact center solution with a rich set of features but without a coherent experience. Current competitors were doing better in the cloud market with better leverage of emerging technologies. The ask for the UX team was to do some blue-sky thinking and come up with an end-to end.

Solution

The UX team came up with a narrative to define some key touch points for the 3 main stakeholders in our ecosystem - the administrator, the agent and the supervisor. We conducted a weeklong brainstorming exercise as a team and stitched a storyline around Air NewZealand and it's flight booking experience.

Project Info

Time: 1 month
Theme: Brainstorming, Storytelling
Contribution: I was primarily responsible for coming up with ideas on the agent side of the story. I also helped in coming up with an overall theme of the narrative and stitching a coherent story around all the features we discussed during the design sprint.

A TLDR version of the project

The project was part of a UX team effort to define the vision for our contact center product for the near future.

The final output was a keynote deck thatw as presented to the leadership and was very weel received.

It spawned two major efforts - Desktop Re-design and Flow Control which the team is currently working on and getting into implementation.

What propelled this effort?

Due to a change in leadership, we had our new VP come up with a new vision statement highlighting a set of 4 pillars for the new generation of contact center solutions we were trying to build. The 4 pillars were viz. Reactive, Contextual, Suggestive and Predictive.

Reactive

React to customer issues as soon as possible

Contextual

Respond to customers with contextual information

Suggestive

Provide suggestions based on data whenever possible

Proactive

Proactively solve customer issues

The Road to the final solution

We followed a 1 week sprint to reach a point where we had doumented all the ideas and the overall storyline so that each one of us could take some responsbility of the final deck and work remotely as we had designers from three different locations.



Setting the landscape
We initially documented the current landscape of our product and its various touchpoints This helped set the baseline and give us a an understanding of the current gaps

Secondary Research
We looked at some of the emerging trends in the market both in terms of technology as well as from our competitor's standpoint. This helped give us a sense of possibilities, what we need to compete with and where we need to innovate.

The Key Personas
Although there are several personas in the contact center ecosystem, for simplicity, we identified the 3 main personas that would help drive the visioning and brainstorming exercise. This was necessary to help keep a coherent flow to the story.

Ideation
We initially started off with several ideas along multiple categories and the objective was to go as crazy as possible. Each one of us came up with as many ideas possible for eeach of the 3 personas and wrote it down in sticky notes.

Thematic Clustering
Next, we all took turns to sahre our ideas with the team and stick our ideas on the whiteboard. If there were similar ideas we started a clustering process to identify over-arching themes and patterns

Feasibility Analysis
We analyzed the ideas against a impact-feasibility matrix to identify some key ideas that we thought would be relevant to implement in our solution for a competitive edge.

Journey Mapping
We finally took the key ideas that we narrowed down upon and stitched it together in acoherent end-to-end journey which eventually transformed into the narrative.

Some Key project outcomes

The narrative became a great rallying artifact around which a lot of important decisions were taken with regards to the product direction. High level executives were keen on implementing at least a couple of the ideas that were thought through. Two key projects which emerged from this efforts are the Desktop Re-design and Flow Control work, both of which have been documented separately. The keynote became a reference point for a lot of design and product decisions and still acts as the Nort Star we all want to head towards!

Learnings from this project

This was a wonderful thought exercise to get the entire team do some unconstrained blue sky-thinking which helped us come up with a lot of novel ideas. In everyday work settings we are often stuck with some fundamental assumptions because of persistently working on a particular product. This exercise actes as a much needed refresh button. It was also imperative to eventually define solutions which are feasible in some capacity and thus some level of grounding was required once the ideation phase was completed. The double-diamond approach helped us in scoping down our ideas. The entire act of connecting all the dots and putting together a coherent story was tough but exhilarating! Last but not the last, it was a great team building exercise as designers from different parts of the world worked seamlessly in a 1 week sprint to come up with several ideas and an overall storyline which we all worked together remotely over the next few weeks. This remains one of my favorite projects of all time.
